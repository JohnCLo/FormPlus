A Crowdsourced Civil Initiative

This project was inspired by the “Photograph and Rescue Child Beggars” initiative implemented by Professor Yu Jianrong of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences Rural Development. Any individual, even you, can participate in helping the less fornuate with a simple photo, location, description, and if possible a name.

You, yes you, can make a difference

The map shows where child beggars have been spotted in China. This data is collected, filed, and forwarded to the Public Security Bureau and relevant NGOs on the framework of an Open Data Initiative to rescue victims of child trafficking. Together we can help victims of child trafficking.

1. Photograph

If possible please provide a clear picture of the child's face in order to aid identification. This website supports photo upload from computers or the newest Apple IOS update for iPhone or IPad devices.

2. Locate

If you are using a smartphone at the site of the spotting please press 'allow geolocation' to mark your current location. If you are reporting from a computer please select the most accurate possible location of the spotting on the map.

3. Report

If possible also ask for a name to aid identification. The entire process takes less than 30 seconds any help is appreciated in locating and assisting victims of child trafficking.

报告瞄准

Data Limitations

If you are experiencing difficulties loading the map data you may either currently have a slow internet connection or Google is at the moment blocked in China. We use Google Spreadsheets to collect, organize, and distribute all reports in a fashion that promotes open data and accessibility. If data access becomes too difficult we plan to eventually move all data to dedicated servers instead of depending on Google Spreadsheets.
